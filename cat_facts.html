Some facts on cats

The cat, often called the domestic cat, is typically a small, furry, carnivorous mammal.

Cats can hear sounds too faint or too high in frequency for human ears, such as those made by mice and other small animals.

Cats can see in near darkness, and like most other mammals, cats have poorer color vision.

Since cats were venerated in ancient Egypt, it is commonly believed that cats were domesticated there

A group of French archaeologists led by Jean-Denis Vigne discovered there may have been instances of domestication as early as the Neolithic from around 7500 BC.

Unlike other domesticated animals, cats probably domesticated themselves.

The International Cat Association currently recognizes 71 breeds of cats for championship competition
